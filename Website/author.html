The Author

Richard Wagamese was an Ojibway author and journalist born in the Wabaseemoong Independent Nations in Northwestern Ontario. The Ojibway are part of the larger Anishinaabe group of Indigenous peoples, whose traditional territories are in the Great Lakes area of North America. Ojibway culture includes the very strong tradition of storytelling, drumming, and the Midewiwin pathway of their spirituality. Saul Indian Horse

Saul is an Ojibway boy taken into a residential school and develops from a traumatized child into a man who confronts his painful past. Through his journey, he learns the importance of acknowledging his trauma and the healing power reconnected to his cultural roots.


Father Leboutilier

A complex character who, at first glance, appears to be someone good but is later revealed to have been part of the agents that caused Saul's trauma.


Fred Kelly

The man who takes Saul in as his own and is like a father, showing him the love and care that he never received as a child.
